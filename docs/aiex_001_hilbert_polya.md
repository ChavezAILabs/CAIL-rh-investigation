## Structural Parallels That Generate This Conjecture

### 1. The nature of the zero

In both systems, the zero is not incidental — it is the defining event.

- **Sedenion zero divisors:** A bilateral pair (P, Q) satisfies P×Q = 0 AND Q×P = 0. Complete mutual annihilation. The Canonical Six are the minimal generating set of all such pairs in 16D sedenion space.
- **Riemann nontrivial zeros:** Points where the zeta function — encoding the entire prime distribution — collapses to zero. Complete cancellation of an infinite sum.

Both are structured, non-trivial zeros that carry deep information about an underlying system.

### 2. The E8 connection

- The Canonical Six P-vector images lie on the E8 lattice first shell and form a single Weyl orbit — formally proven in Lean 4 (v1.3, February 2026).
- The Riemann zeros have been speculatively connected to E8 via the Langlands program, where L-functions generalizing the Riemann zeta function connect to automorphic representations of exceptional Lie groups, including E8.

Same exceptional structure. Two paths in.

### 3. The Montgomery-Dyson coincidence

In 1972, Hugh Montgomery (number theory) and Freeman Dyson (quantum physics) discovered at a tea party that the pair correlation of Riemann zeta zeros matches the pair correlation of eigenvalues in a Gaussian Unitary Ensemble — the same random matrix model describing energy levels of heavy atomic nuclei.

The zeros governing prime distribution behave like a quantum system. The Hilbert-Pólya conjecture says they *are* the eigenvalues of an undiscovered self-adjoint quantum operator.

That operator has never been found.

### 4. The sedenion annihilation topology

The Canonical Six generate a 24-element bilateral zero divisor family via P-vector and Q-vector cross-products. The annihilation events — where 16D algebraic objects collapse to zero — have internal structure (Type I instant vs. Type II delayed annihilation, documented in separate research memo, March 3, 2026) that maps onto a dynamics framework.

A self-adjoint operator whose eigenvalues are encoded in annihilation events in 16D sedenion space is not obviously impossible.
