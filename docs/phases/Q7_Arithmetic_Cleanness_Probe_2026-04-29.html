1. Background

The Sedenion Horizon Sweep (Phase 72, April 26) discovered under sparse encoding [σ, γ, σ−½, 0,…,0] that σ=½ produces exact integer coordinates in the 32D ZDTP gateway lift while off-critical σ produces fractional coordinates (AIEX-575). The question was whether this was:

  • An artifact of the sparse encoding, where σ−½ = 0 at the critical line zeroes out a coordinate
  • A deeper structural property — provable from the Hamiltonian H(s) = (Re(s)−½)·u_antisym

This probe answers by testing under full F(s) prime exponential encoding, where the σ−½ sparsity argument cannot apply.

4. Analysis

Arithmetic cleanness is encoding-independent. The 2σ = 1.0 result at σ=½ holds under full F(s) prime exponential encoding — the same encoding used in the Phase 73 spectral run. The sparse encoding argument (σ−½=0 zeroes a coordinate) cannot explain the cleanness under F(s) inputs, where no such zero appears. The phenomenon is structural.

Arithmetic cleanness is provable from H(s). The Sedenionic Hamiltonian H(s) = (Re(s)−½)·u_antisym has the factor (Re(s)−½) as the generator of all σ-dependent structure in the 32D lift. When Re(s)=½ this factor is zero — H(s)=0 — and the σ-dependent coordinates collapse to their unit-normalized values (1.0, given ‖u_antisym‖²=2). The 2σ coordinate scaling is a direct consequence.

The critical line is geometrically unique in the 32D lift. σ=½ is the unique value where 2σ=1 — integer-exact. Off-critical σ produces rational (but non-integer) coordinates that scale linearly with distance from ½. This is the geometric fingerprint of H(s)=0 in the lifted space, and provides empirical grounding for the Phase 73 eigenvalue-zero mapping theorem.

Gateway-independence confirmed. The 2σ law holds for both S1 (Class B) and S2 (Class A), confirming it is not specific to the ± structure of the zero-divisor pattern. It is a universal property of the Hamiltonian's action in the 32D lift.
